## Core model

An ecotourism node is a territorial circuit that combines:
- lodging with a reliable minimum standard of comfort
- local experiences and service providers
- visitor-facing coordination and support
- progressive infrastructure improvements
- public documentation and replicability

### Guiding principle
The circuit does not replace local offerings. It makes them more visible, coordinated, and sellable.

### Secondary principle
The circuit begins with what already exists and progressively improves infrastructure and coordination.

### Resilience principle
The system should integrate many actors while making **none of them indispensable**.

### Territorial principle
The value created should remain increasingly in the territory: infrastructure, visibility, reputation, know-how, articulated demand, and future lodging capacity.

### Replicability principle
The model should be documented openly and simply so other zones can adapt and replicate it.

---

## Why this exists

Many places with high natural, cultural, and scenic value do not capture more tourism because they lack a workable combination of:
- reliable lodging
- clearly articulated local activities and services
- minimum support infrastructure
- simple visitor-facing coordination
- support for visitors who do not know the area, the local language, the local payment methods, or the local provider ecosystem

### Mallasa-specific problem
In Mallasa, tourism is also highly imbalanced:
- weekends attract many local visitors
- tourism drops sharply during the week

One goal of the pilot is to help activate more valuable and coordinated tourism during the week as well.

## Lodging philosophy

### The RV
The RV is an **initial enabler**, not the end goal.

It is used first because it can offer a reliable minimum comfort standard where that standard may not yet exist.

For current documentation and communication, an RV should be explained simply as:
> a mobile home / casa rodante with bed, bathroom, shower, kitchen, and basic comfort services

Operationally, current assumption:
- approximate autonomy: **up to 3 days**, depending on use and logistics

### Future lodging openness
The system should already be conceptually open to future lodging formats, especially if proposed by local actors, including:
- camping
- hosted stays in local homes / casas de familias / anfitriones locales
- other mobile units
- simple local lodging formats
- future fixed accommodations

This openness matters so the project does not feel designed only around the current RV.

### Important framing
The RV is the initial comfort bridge where infrastructure is limited. The broader project is the circuit.

## Current operating phases

Current first operating version of the circuit:
- visitors stay at **Proyecto Cultural Barranco**
- or in the **RV at / next to Barranco**
- local experiences are layered on top of that stay
- this phase validates pricing, narrative, operations, and local coordination
- it also creates pilot records and data

This phase is important because it allows the model to start **before** the towing vehicle exists.

### Phase 2: after towing vehicle
Tentative vehicle timeline:
- **June / July**

Once towing becomes available, the circuit can expand toward:
- the horse / riding areas
- the Muela del Diablo area
- linked routes by walking, cycling, e-bike, horse, etc.
- the possibility that the lodging unit waits at the next point in the circuit

Long-term experiential aspiration:
The area should be felt more like a small natural park with trails, activities, landscapes, and multiple ways of staying in the territory.

## Economics: current model

### Key principle
For now, economics should be realistic rather than dreamy.

The immediate objective is to make the project float, validate demand, and create records.

### Exchange rate assumption
Internal working rate:
- **1 USD = 9 Bs**

This is used because the real market rate matters more for this project than the official rate.

### Airbnb fee model assumption
Use the realistic **host-only / single-fee Airbnb model** where roughly **15.5%** is deducted from the host payout.

This fee also affects cleaning fees and host fees.

### Early discount assumption
The initial Airbnb listing may accept early discounted first stays because current RV revenue is zero and early reviews are strategically valuable.

### Initial “psychological” pricing idea
A simple psychological model discussed:
- **$100/night visible to guest**
- **$100 cleaning fee visible to guest**

This is attractive psychologically, but must be evaluated against the true payout after Airbnb fees and current cost structure.

### Realistic pricing discussion
When the full towing vehicle cost was loaded into the current single-unit model, the required nightly price became too high.

This led to the important conclusion that:
- the towing vehicle should **not** be fully amortized by a single RV in the pilot
- the pilot RV should only carry its fair share / use share of vehicle deployment
- the rest should be justified via future multi-unit logic or deployment subsidy

### Current revised floor logic
Removing full vehicle amortization from the nightly model and keeping only the RV-related costs plus local site fee produced a more realistic floor in the range of roughly **$100/night visible to guest**, though exact formulas may evolve.

### Local site contribution
A working assumption used:
- **50 Bs/night** contribution to local site / location host (e.g. horse area / future campground)

This must remain part of the formula.

### Minimal fixed monthly placeholder
A minimal extra placeholder of **1500 Bs/month** was kept in the model.
This could later grow into a fixed managerial / coordination salary when the system has more units.

### Minimum wage reference
Bolivia minimum wage reference used for delegation thinking:
- **3300 Bs/month**

This matters when thinking about paying a turnover operator enough for the role to become delegable.

## Cleaning fee and turnover logic

### Current cleaning fee decision
Current working cleaning fee:
- **400 Bs**

### What CF is intended to cover initially
- real cleaning (~100 Bs assumption)
- moving the RV (~100 Bs assumption)
- picking it up (~100 Bs assumption)
- some gasoline
- partial vehicle amortization in the future
- operator / driver compensation in the future

### Mid-stay service
At the current stage, mid-stay service may initially be priced similarly to the cleaning fee because operationally it still requires:
- taking the RV back to base (Barranco)
- handling drainage / refill
- returning and resetting it

In the future, a mid-stay service could become cheaper once closer water / drainage infrastructure exists.

### Important constraint
At the moment, there is no local dump / refill point at the destination area and no easy way to bring fresh water there, which is why service still implies heavy logistics.

## Towing vehicle logic

### Current projected vehicle
A likely towing vehicle is a **Honda Ridgeline AWD**, tentatively available via leasing around June / July.

### Strategic conclusion
The vehicle should be understood as shared deployment infrastructure, not as a fixed cost to fully assign to one current RV.

### Long-term scalable logic
The vehicle becomes more rational economically when it serves:
- multiple lodging units
- multiple spots
- multiple turnovers

### Deployment subsidy idea
The gap during the pilot can be framed as **deployment subsidy** rather than charity.

This is fundable because the vehicle / mobility layer helps create:
- access to new lodging spots
- infrastructure learning
- local capacity
- a model that can later support multiple units and multiple zones

- towing vehicle access
- closer water refill / drainage points
- signage

- lighting / basic support infrastructure
- operational systems that reduce costs and increase autonomy

This is best understood as investment in infrastructure and deployment capacity that remains useful to the territory.

### Future financing logic

- leasing / access to towing vehicle

## What should remain in the territory

The project should increasingly leave behind:
- useful infrastructure
- signage and trail/circuit improvements
- documented know-how
- destination visibility and reputation
- articulated demand for local actors
- future local lodging capacity
- increasing resilience without dependence on a single actor

### Important nuance
It is better to say:
> the value created should remain progressively in the territory

rather than claiming absolutely that “everything stays in the zone” from day one.
