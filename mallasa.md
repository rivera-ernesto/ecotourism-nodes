# Circuito Ecoturístico Mallasa

*First pilot of Ecotourism Nodes*

[Español](es/mallasa.md)

## One-line summary

Circuito Ecoturístico Mallasa is the first pilot of Ecotourism Nodes: a territorial circuit that combines reliable lodging, local experiences, and gradual infrastructure improvements to activate higher-quality ecotourism in Mallasa.

## Why Mallasa

Mallasa already has strong natural, cultural, and recreational value, but much of its tourism is concentrated on weekends and does not always translate into longer stays or a more coordinated local economy.

The pilot starts from a simple premise: if visitors can stay comfortably and access local experiences more easily, more value can circulate within the area.

## Local problems being addressed

- low midweek tourism compared with weekends
- fragmented local offerings
- barriers for visitors who do not know the area
- language and coordination barriers
- payment friction with local providers
- lack of enough lodging with a reliable comfort standard
- limited support infrastructure for expanded stays

## Initial minimum circuit

Until a towing vehicle is available, the first operating version of the circuit is centered on Proyecto Cultural Barranco, located in Mallasa at Las Tunas 224.

The initial circuit includes:

- stays at Proyecto Cultural Barranco
- the possibility of staying inside Barranco or in the RV outside
- local experiences and services from the area
- mapping of both paid and free attractions
- testing of pricing, operations, narrative, and visitor experience

## Why start with an RV

The pilot starts with an RV because it can provide a minimum standard of comfort from day one.

An RV is a mobile home with bed, bathroom, shower, kitchen, and basic comfort services. In practice, the unit can operate with an approximate autonomy of up to 3 days, depending on usage and available logistics.

This makes it possible to test tourism activation in places where comfort infrastructure may still be limited.

## Expansion phase

Once a towing vehicle is available, tentatively around June or July, the circuit can expand toward:

- stays in the horse and riding areas
- stays in the Muela del Diablo area
- linked routes through walking, cycling, e-bikes, horses, and other modes
- the possibility of moving the lodging unit to the next point in the circuit

The longer-term aspiration is for the area to be experienced more like a small natural park, with trails, activities, landscapes, and multiple ways of staying within the territory.

## Actors that may be integrated

- Proyecto Cultural Barranco
- local horse and riding associations
- guides and local activity providers
- restaurants and local food offerings
- tourism agencies operating in Mallasa
- future campground hosts
- future local home-stay hosts
- future sponsors or deployment backers

## Seed operator

In its initial stage, Ernesto Rivera, a Mallasa resident since 1983, acts as the initial seed operator of the pilot.

This role includes both coordination and deployment.

## What the pilot seeks to leave behind

The goal is for the value created by the circuit to remain increasingly in Mallasa and its surroundings, including:

- useful infrastructure for the area
- signage and circuit improvements
- documented operational learning
- greater visibility and reputation for the destination
- better articulated demand for local actors
- future local lodging capacity
- increasing resilience without dependence on a single actor

## Deployment and financing logic

The pilot can start in an austere and manual way while real demand is being validated.

At the same time, it can help build a financeable case for deployment needs such as:

- a towing vehicle
- closer water refill and drainage points
- signage and spot enabling
- improvements that reduce operating costs and increase autonomy

This allows the project to frame external support not as charity, but as deployment support for territorial tourism capacity.

## Replicability

Mallasa is not intended as a closed or centralized model.

It is the first pilot of a broader open logic: ecotourism nodes that can be documented, adapted, and replicated in other territories with strong natural or cultural value.

## Current stage

- initial pilot centered on Proyecto Cultural Barranco
- towing vehicle targeted for June/July
- public documentation in progress
